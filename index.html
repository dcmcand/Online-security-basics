Online Security Basics

"By the end of this class, you will feel less secure and be more secure"
- Blake Carver

Security is not binary. Something is more secure or less secure. It is wrong to say something is or is not secure.

1. Do Your Updates
    Most hacks involve known vulnerabilities
    Equifax hack used vulnerability that was patched months ago
    Auto updates ensure that updates get applied
    Software makers generally do well patching security vulnerabilities, but if you don't update, it doesn't matter
2. If you can remember all of your passwords, you are doing it wrong
    LassPass estimates that 85% of account compromises are because of poor passwords
    Every account should have strong, unique password
    Credential stuffing attacks use compromised credentials on all popular websites
    Password Managers are the best way to deal with this
      LastPass
      Bitwarden
      KeePass
      1Password
3. A Long Password is a Strong Password
    Adding length to a password makes it much harder to guess than adding complexity
    Passphrases are better than passwords
4. Don't rely on passwords
    Two Factor Authentication (2FA) uses something you know (your password) and something you have (a cell phone normally) or something you are (biometrics)
    2FA should be used everywhere possible, especially on email
    keep backup codes safe in case your phone is lost or stolen
    Avoid SMS 2FA if possible (sometimes it isn't possible). SMS 2FA is better than nothing but worse than everything else
5. If you don't back it up, you don't care about it
    3,2,1 Rule
      3 Copies of data
      2 different media types
      1 copy off site
    Time machine/shadow copy can be used for local backup, but aren't full backup solutions by themselves
    Backblaze, Carbonite, and other online solutions can be very good for offsite backups
6. Backups are worthless, restores are priceless
    2017 Gitlab lost hours of customer data because none of their 5 backup strategies worked
7. Send letters, not postcards
8. Practice defensive browsing
9. Don't trust public wifi
10. Don't trust email
